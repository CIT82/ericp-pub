"Characters poses can reveal much about them, down to what emotions they are feeling, poses are an incredibly important element of drawing"

He explained this in 2017 in his book Manga in Theory and Practice: The Craft of Creating Manga

What inspired him to do this?

Hirohiko Araki love for his reference is beautiful. The way he shows the art, pop culuture, art, and fashion. He had remained consintent through decades of his work. He has love for fashion of all kinds. From Versace, Moschino, and his heavy usage of Vogue magazine photoshots. Not onyl does he references that but also works of art, music, cultural references, flims, western influences, and personal experiences.

Why does he do it?

He loves it. His work stands out in the world of manga, he creates work that will be rememembered and recognize. I feel this adds authenticity to his way of story telling but also reflects his appreciation for the references he uses. He applies this to the global audience, for everyone that can find elements of the manga in which will resonate with the reader. He has been relevant through his whole decades of work, it is working and it helps with avoiding repetition because it is never ending.
