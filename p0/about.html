Hirohiko Araki

Hirohiko Araki, born in 1960 in Wakabayashi Ward, Sendai, Miyagi Japan, is a distugished individual with a wealth of experience and accomplishments. He is professoional in his way of work and his art work is greatly respected by others. Araki Hirohiko is married to Asami Araki and together they are proud parents of two daughters.

Renowned for his illustrious career, Hirohiko Araki stands as a paragon of success within the realm of manga creation. Widely acclaimed for his work on,"Jojo Bizarre Adventure", which made its debut in Weekly Shonen Jump in 1987, his creative prowess has left a indelible mark on the manga landscape. Boasting an impressive circulation of 120 million copies. Making it one of the best selling manga series in history.

Araki was selected as one of five distinguished artists by the Musee du Louvre to craft original works set within the hallowed halls of the renowned museum. His masterpiece, "Rohan at the Louvre," captivated audiences with its unique fusion of JoJo elements and classical artistry. One of the standout collaborations in Araki's career unfolded at the Gucci store in Shinjuku from September 17 to October 6, 2011. There are plenty more but I would say these two are my personal favorite.
